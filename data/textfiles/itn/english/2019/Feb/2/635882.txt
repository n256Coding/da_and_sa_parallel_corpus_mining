A half-built fairytale development of hundreds of Disney-style castles in Western Turkey won a reprieve from bankruptcy when creditors voted to allow a construction company to complete work on the project.The Burj el Babas project, about 200 km east of Istanbul, was intended to be a luxury housing development boasting rows upon rows of cream-coloured castles with grey turrets, all built around a shopping mall and hotel.
But delays in obtaining construction permits pushed up building costs, as the falling lira drove up prices of building materials.
